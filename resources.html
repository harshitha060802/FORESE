Tips To Crack an Interview


Interviews are a vital component in the hiring process. With each job interview, we meet new people, we display our skills, stay upbeat and remain enthusiastic through it all. This can be a challenge, especially when you're interviewing for a job you would love to get hired for!! Glance through these tips to get through almost any interview and make the process look sublime.

Research about the company

Do significant research about the company you are planning to interview for before even applying. Before the interview, knowing about the foundation of the company.

Reach early to the location

Punctuality is very important for the interview. Being timely are a few things you must practice in the standard of living because it can always help you.

Be confident in your body language

Win them over with your authenticity and positivity. A confident candidate is always preferred even if the other candidate had a better-looking resume.

Decorum To Be Maintained During Mock Placements



Online Mocks

  • Make sure you have a good internet connection.
  • Make sure your camera is turned on and the mic is working.
  • Greet the HR once you join the meeting. Do not use colloquial language while talking to the HR.
  • Students must login only through their laptops and not through any other devices.
  • The resume should not exceed one page, follow the resume format given and it should have credible information.
  • PDF version of your resume must be kept ready for screen sharing during the interview.
  • Only students who have attended the GD and aptitude test will be allowed to sit for the Mock Placements.
  • Make sure you listen to the instructions given by your student in charge.

Offline Mocks

  • Be punctual to the interview. Show up to the interview at least 5 minutes before the allotted time.
  • Adhere strictly to the stipulated dress code - stick to professional attire.
  • Address the interviewer in a polite, courteous manner. Be very respectful and polite to the HR.
  • Come adequately prepared for the interview - bring all necessary materials such as a resume and other documents.
  • Keep your answers brief, succinct, impersonal and to the point.
  • Similarly, keep your queries to the interviewer impersonal and relevant to the topic at hand.
  • Come thoroughly well-researched on your company so that you can ask related questions and make a positive impression on the interviewer.
  • Always be calm. Any doubts can be addressed to the student in-charge.

Tips to Make a Resume


A professionally designed resume is a gateway to your career. Resume building is an art and science. Use these attention grabbing strategies to make the recruiter pick your resume (you only have ten seconds to stand a chance in the eyes of the HR)

Pitch a clear and crisp profile

Provide a professional photo in a formal/semi formal attire with a smile. Use Key terms and meta data so that your resume is discoverable by not just HR but also AI bots.

Highlight your key strengths

Prioritize highlighting your most significant accomplishments and credentials. Include any electives and specialties in addition to your main course of study.

Keep it personal and professional

Your resume should reflect your personality and stay unique as well as present professional achievements and include distinctive hobbies.

Other Professional work

Include any projects you've worked on, as well as any seminars, publications, or conferences you've attended.

Go beyond academic achievements

Show that you are a multifaceted individual by listing your club and sport activities in addition to your academic achievements.

Establish social media presence

Build a LinkedIn page and get connected with professionals, as well as being active on Instagram and Twitter to remain updated.
